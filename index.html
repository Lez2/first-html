Tastes of The World

Around the World in 30 Bites

Written by: Tatianna Jordan and Leslie Nut-Komlah

Let’s face it, our need for food goes far beyond the nourishment for our bodies. Food bring people together; It expresses hospitality and eases social interactions. It serves as a cultural expression woven into the fabric of every community. Life crisis, holidays, and rites of passage are often marked by the exchange and consumption of foods. Here is a small taste of what the world has to offer.

Africa

Africa is rich in diverse culture and spices. Each country has their own story to go along with their unique and authentic recipes.

Kushari

Kushari is a simple, yet flavorful Egytptian dish consisting of rice, pasta, and lentils. Before serving, it is topped with wine vinegar and a spicy sauce with either a tomator or chili base, adding a hearty base to the dish. Some people even choose to add crunch, fried vermicelli and butter-browned onions.

Banku and Tilapia

Banku and Tilapia is a coastal dish that is a delicacy to the people of Ghana. Banku is a mixture of fermented corn and cassava dough prepared over a stove. A combinationof a pepper blend, chopped onions, and tomatoes are essiantial to the meal. The well-spiced, grilled tilapia sets the dish apart from all others.

Asia

Asia is know for many diverse and distiguished cuisines that are loved worldwide.

Dan Dan Noodles

Dan Dan Noodles are a dish that is served in China. It is usally served with spicy sauce, but it may be replaced with sesame paste or peanut butter if "Americanized".

Gyoza

Gyoza is a well known Japanese dish served throughout the world. It's flavorful ingredients include ginger, green onion, cabbage, and your choice of meat stuffed in a wheat flour wrap. These dumplings are folded in a crescent shape and are usually bite sized.

Europe

When many people think of Europe they think of the Queen of England around teatime, well althrough this isn't completely false, many more dishes that we love today originatred in a European Country. But we would like to focus on more tradition dishes.

Coq Au Vin

Coq Au Vin is a traditional stew hailing from Burgundy. Chicken is cut into sections and combined with onions, carrots, garlic, thyme, bay leaves, parsley, mushrooms, salt, pepper, and a hefty dose of red wine. The ingredients are then slowly cooked over low heat until the meat becomes tender.

Seafood Paella

Seafood Paella is a savory rice dish from Spain. The dish is often referred to as a staple in Spanish cuisine. Paella is great for large gatherings because it can be served to many people. The seafood, includiong mussles, clams and shrimp, is often served in their shells. It is perfectly paired with a pitcher of sangria.

Australia

Australia has a huge multicultural population that is a mixing pot of different dishes from different people. Here are what locals often refer to as Australian cuisine!

Lamingtons

Lamingtons are sponge cakes that are dipped in chocolate and sprinkled with finely grated coconut. The Queensland native dish is a sweet treat that is enjoyed by everyone who tries it. It is a staple of fundraising events due to the fact that it can be easily made in large quantities.

Barramundi

Barramundi is a versatile Australian fish that can be steamed, baked, fried, or grilled. One favorite is Sautéed Barramundi with Lemon Butter Sauce. It is garnished with basil and served with a side of mashed potatoes or chopped tomatoes.

North America

North America is has many tantalizing dishes that are staples in their own coutries.

Lobster Rolls

This easy yet creative dish had is start in New England, Maryland. The lobster roll is a sandwhich that consists of diced lobster meat put in between a grilled, split top, on a hoydog bun. There's usually flavor added to the lobster by adding mayo if you would like it served cold, or a butter sauce if you would like it served warm.

South America

South America has a rich culture of bringing people together through food. Whether it be from food vendors or from the comfort of someone's home, food is a major part of their culture.

anticuchos

Anticuchos is a savory street food dish served on a stick in peru. The meat most commonly put on skewers is beef heart, which is marinated in red wine vinegar, garlic, and aji pepper. This dish can be enjoyed anytime, anywhere, and is frequently sold by street carts called Anticucheras during festivals. Next time you find yourself in Peru, do yourself a favor and buy a heart on a stick!

Pamplona de Pollo

Orginating from Uruguay, Pamplona de Pollo is rolled chicken breast stuffed with ham, smoked bacon, bell peppers and cheese. Each dish is stuffed differently depending on the chef.
